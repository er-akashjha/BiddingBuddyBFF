02 The framing

"Compete with GeM" is three products, not one

AmbitionLegal positionBuildVerdict
Replace GeM for central-government common-use goods Blocked — GFR 149 makes GeM the mandated channel — Do not pursue
e-Publishing — host and distribute tender notices Open today. No certification: we never receive a bid ~11 wks Approve now
e-Procurement — receive, seal, open, evaluate bids Legal and licensed under GFR 160 12–18 mo Gate B

They differ by two orders of magnitude in cost and risk. Collapsing them was the original error.

03 Regulatory reality

A private e-procurement portal is lawful — and the market already exists

160The GFR rule permitting a department to use "a service provider of their own choice"
22Private e-procurement systems holding live STQC certification today
2012DoE O.M. requiring departments to hold an STQC certificate for the solution they use
The obligation lands on the buyer, not on us

There is no statute licensing e-procurement software. The duty sits with the procuring officer, who discharges it by demanding certification from their vendor. Nothing prohibits us from selling — the requirement is that our customer can defend the choice in audit.

Incumbents include mjunction, C1 India, nCode/GNFC and abcprocure. This is not a question of permission. It is a question of whether we have anything they don't.

04 Why us

We start on the far side of the cold-start problem

Every new e-procurement portal dies the same way: departments won't publish where there are no bidders, and bidders won't join where there are no tenders.

The asset nobody else has

We already operate the bidder side — a scraped national corpus, a supplier base, and a live matching and alerting rail. A department publishing with us reaches suppliers on day one. Incumbents sell a portal; we can sell reach.

Two further advantages: zero-install experience (they carry a decade of desktop-coupled architecture) and compliance-by-construction.

05 Differentiation

We are not selling efficiency. We are selling defensibility.

01

Zero install

CPPP requires a Class 3 certificate on a hardware token, two key pairs per officer, and its published requirements still reference Java and legacy browsers.

GeM already accepts eSign with no dongle. The old stack is an implementation choice, not a legal one.

02

Compliance by construction

MSE reservation, Make-in-India local content, land-border registration, Integrity Pact thresholds — today checked by hand against circulars.

We encode them as blocking validation, with the rule citation written into the audit file.

03

The off-GeM justification

A department procuring outside GeM must produce an availability report justifying it.

We can generate it from our own GeM corpus. It is also the document that legitimises our customers' use of us.

Departments fear an audit paragraph considerably more than they want speed. Sell to that.

08 The real cost of STQC

Not a gate. A permanent constraint on how we ship.

  • Valid three years, with surveillance audits after years one and two — in physical mode, not online.
  • Security and vulnerability reports must be no older than six months at every surveillance.
  • "Only major changes which affect core functionality, security or infrastructure will lead to recertification" — a material rearchitecture restarts the clock.
  • Deviations unrectified within three months → certificate withdrawn, and with it every government customer.
Disclosure

We could not locate an official STQC schedule of charges. The guidelines specify only that "one man day audit charges shall be levied," excluding security testing. The ₹3–10L figure is from commercial sources and must not be used for budgeting.

This imposes release governance on the whole engineering organisation, not one service — the strongest argument for deferring Phase 3.

09 Signing & encryption

One solved problem, one unsolved one

CapabilityZero-install possible?Basis
Signing — create, publish, award Yes — proven in production GeM accepts eSign for bid publishing, order creation and invoicing, for buyers and sellers alike.
Bid encryption and decryption No — structurally impossible eSign keys are "destroyed after one time use," certificates valid "not more than 30 minutes." A bid opened three weeks later cannot use one.
Unresolved — gates the Phase 3 design

STQC states that only Class 3 certificates may be used in a certified system. eSign issues e-KYC class certificates — so on a literal reading eSign is excluded from the regulated product, removing our principal experience advantage.

We assess this as likely an artefact of vintage: the governing guidelines are dated 31 August 2011, four years older than eSign itself. A written clarification from STQC is the highest-value item in Phase 0.

For decryption, copy MeitY's own published GeM design: Class 3 key moved off the token into an HSM, released by PIN and OTP, split so any two of three holders can open a bid.

10 Commercial model

Four revenue routes, one of which we must refuse

RouteMechanismAssessment
Entity subscriptionAnnual licence per entity, tiered by volumePredictable, matches government budget cycles. Recommended primary.
Per-tender feeTransaction fee per notice publishedSuits low-volume entities that cannot justify a licence.
Compliance add-onsAI drafting, availability reports, audit exportHighest margin, unique to us, clearest upsell.
Bidder processing feeCharge suppliers to download or submitReject
Strategic conflict — management must rule

The dominant model in this industry is to tax the bidder. Our existing business is the bidder. Adopting it would monetise the buyer side by degrading the constituency our current revenue depends on. We recommend a standing public commitment never to charge bidders.

No revenue forecast is offered. We have no pricing research and no conversion data; a projection built on neither would be decoration. Establishing willingness to pay is Ask C.

14 Recommendation

Buy information cheaply before buying the platform expensively

Phase 1 costs roughly eleven person-weeks, requires no certification, generates revenue, and produces exactly the three things Gate B needs.

  • Real departments on the platform
  • Real willingness-to-pay data
  • A written answer from STQC

If Phase 1 fails to attract entities, we will have learned that for the price of a quarter of one engineer's year — rather than for the price of a certified platform.
